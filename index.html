Nature of Sri Lanka



Sri Lanka is the Pearl of the Indian Ocean and teardrop of India.



Sri Lanka is home to approximately ninety-one species of mammals. Of these ninety-one, sixteen are endemic and can only be found in Sri Lanka while fourteen are threatened including the large Sloth Bear, the Sri Lankan Leopard and the Sri Lankan Elephant. Due to the geographical differences (climate, vegetation and altitude) found in Sri Lanka, mammals are widely distributed throughout the country.

The Sloth Bear and Leopards are found in the majority of National Parks in Sri Lanka, however, Sloth Bears are most commonly spotted between June and July either Yala or Wasgamuwa National Parks. Research suggests that Yala National Park hosts the highest density of leopards in the world.

Sri Lanka is home to 171 reptile species, the majority of which are snakes. The largest reptiles in Sri Lanka are two species of crocodile, the Mugger Crocodile and the Saltwater Crocodile. With 106 amphibian species, many claims that Sri Lanka has the highest density of amphibian species in the world.




What is the nature of Sri Lanka?

Sri Lanka is a tropical island situated close to the southern tip of India. The island measures about 65,610 square kilometers (25,332 square miles), with 1,340 kilometers (832 miles) of coastline. The island is covered with tropical rainforest, plains, and highlands. The central part of the island is mountainous with two main peaks, Pidurutalagala and Kirigalpotta, both reaching over 2,500 meters (8,202 feet) in height. The highest point in Sri Lanka is Pidurutalagala, which is 2,524 meters (8,281 feet) tall.

What are the main features of the nature of Sri Lanka?

The island is home to many types of vegetation, including tropical rainforests, mangroves, and grasslands. The island is also home to many types of animals, including elephants, leopards, and monkeys. The island is also home to many types of birds, including parrots, peacocks, and hornbills. The island is also home to many types of fish, including tuna, mackerel, and sardines. The island is also home to many types of reptiles, including snakes, lizards, and crocodiles.

What are the main attractions of the nature of Sri Lanka?

The island is home to many natural attractions, including national parks, botanical gardens, and waterfalls. The island is also home to many cultural attractions, including ancient ruins, temples, and festivals. The island is also home to many adventure attractions, including hiking, surfing, and diving. The island is also home to many wellness attractions, including yoga, meditation, and ayurveda.

What are the main threats to the nature of Sri Lanka?

The island is facing many threats to its nature, including deforestation, pollution, and climate change. The island is also facing many threats to its wildlife, including poaching, habitat loss, and human-wildlife conflict. The island is also facing many threats to its marine life, including overfishing, pollution, and climate change. The island is also facing many threats to its cultural heritage, including looting, vandalism, and neglect.

What are the main conservation efforts in Sri Lanka?

The island is home to many conservation efforts, including national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and marine protected areas. The island is also home to many conservation organizations, including the Department of Wildlife Conservation, the Forest Department, and the Central Cultural Fund. The island is also home to many conservation projects, including reforestation, habitat restoration, and species recovery. The island is also home to many conservation initiatives, including eco-tourism, sustainable development, and community engagement.


Sri Lanka's nature is an enchanting blend of diverse ecosystems and stunning landscapes. From the misty hills of the central highlands to the golden shores that fringe its coastline, the island nation offers a wealth of natural beauty.

The central highlands of Sri Lanka are adorned with verdant tea plantations that stretch as far as the eye can see. Nestled among these emerald slopes are cascading waterfalls, such as the iconic Ramboda Falls and the breathtaking Devon Falls. The cool, crisp air of these highlands provides a refreshing escape from the tropical heat, making it a haven for travelers seeking tranquility and natural splendor.

Venturing further, Sri Lanka's national parks beckon with their rich biodiversity. Yala National Park, renowned for its high density of leopards, offers thrilling safari experiences amidst its scrublands and lagoons. Wilpattu National Park, the largest in the country, is a haven for birdwatchers and wildlife enthusiasts, boasting a diverse array of species including Sri Lankan elephants, sloth bears, and elusive leopards.

The island's coastline is equally captivating, with pristine beaches that stretch for miles along the Indian Ocean. From the bustling beaches of Bentota and Unawatuna to the secluded coves of Tangalle and Mirissa, each offers its own unique charm and allure. The turquoise waters teem with marine life, making it a paradise for snorkeling, diving, and whale watching.

From its misty mountains to its sun-kissed beaches, Sri Lanka's nature is a testament to the island's rich biodiversity and breathtaking beauty. Whether exploring its national parks, relaxing on its beaches, or trekking through its jungles, visitors are sure to be captivated by the natural wonders that abound in this tropical paradise.
